3. Positive pole: The positive pole is dominated by robust, full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon wines that exhibit deep, dark fruit flavors, complex spice notes, and a significant tannic structure. These wines are often described as rich, concentrated, and layered, with a focus on dark berries, chocolate, and earthy undertones.
4. Negative pole: The negative pole consists of lighter, more delicate white wines that showcase vibrant acidity, citrus, and stone fruit flavors. These wines are typically described as fresh, crisp, and easy to drink, with a focus on minerality and a clean, straightforward finish.
