3. Positive pole: The positive pole is dominated by rich, wood-aged Portuguese wines with prominent notes of plum, spice, and a full, dense texture. These wines often come from regions like Douro and Península de Setúbal, showcasing a deep, almost opulent character.
4. Negative pole: The negative pole features Italian red wines, such as Brunello di Montalcino and Barolo, which are characterized by their more delicate and earthy aromas. These wines tend to have notes of mature fruit, leather, and a more subtle, herbal complexity.
