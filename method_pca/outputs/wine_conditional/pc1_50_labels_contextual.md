# PCA Component Labels

## PC1

- Explained variance: `4.5187%`

1. **Axis label:** Rich vs. Fresh
2. **Positive pole:** The positive examples emphasize a rich, complex flavor profile with notes of blackberry, anise, and cedar, indicating a fuller-bodied wine with depth and complexity. These wines are often described as having a robust character, with flavors that are concentrated and layered.
3. **Negative pole:** Conversely, the negative examples highlight wines that are fresh, crisp, and acidic, with notes of citrus and green fruits. These wines are characterized by their bright acidity and lighter, more straightforward flavors, suggesting a fresher and more immediate drinking experience.

## PC2

- Explained variance: `4.0798%`

1. Axis label: **Harsh vs. Rounded**
   
2. Positive pole: This direction captures wines that are perceived as harsh or lacking in smoothness. Wines described as having a "scoury" texture, marked by sharp acidity and off-putting aromas like cigarette ash, fall on the positive end of this axis. Examples include the Kitchen Sink NV Winemaker's Reserve Red and Lucky Star 2011 Petite Sirah, both noted for their astringent and unrefined qualities.

3. Negative pole: Conversely, wines that are described as smooth, well-rounded, and enjoyable sit on the negative end of this axis. These wines exhibit a pleasant balance and complexity without the harsh elements. Examples include the Domaines Barons de Rothschild (Lafite) 2010 Val de L'Ours Cabernet Sauvignon-Syrah, which is characterized by its fresh, structured profile, and the Château de Pennautier 2006 Cabernet Sauvignon de Pennautier, known for its classic, well-integrated flavors and sinewy tannins.
