Introduction

You are invited to participate in a research study conducted by {{PI Name}} at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). Please read this form carefully before deciding whether to participate. You must be at least 18 years old to take part.

Purpose of the Study

We are studying how people make judgments in everyday situations involving competing values. The goal is to better understand the dimensions along which people's preferences vary, and to use this understanding to develop AI systems that can learn from human feedback more efficiently.

What You Will Be Asked to Do

If you agree to participate, you will:

  • Read approximately 30 short scenarios, each describing an everyday situation with two possible courses of action.
  • For each scenario, choose which of the two actions you find more reasonable.
  • Depending on the version of the study you receive, you may also be shown brief summaries of inferences about your apparent values after each choice and asked to confirm, adjust, or remove them.

You may decline to answer any individual scenario by selecting "skip" or by withdrawing from the study.

Time Required

The study takes approximately 15–25 minutes to complete.

Risks and Discomforts

The risks of participating are minimal. Some scenarios describe ethically difficult situations (e.g., workplace conflicts, family obligations, environmental tradeoffs). If any scenario makes you uncomfortable, you may skip it or stop the study at any time without penalty.

Benefits

You will be compensated for your time. Your participation contributes to research on aligning AI systems with human values.

Confidentiality and Anonymity

Your responses are anonymous. We will receive only your Prolific Participant ID — never your name, email address, IP address, or any other identifying information. Your data will be stored on MIT-approved Qualtrics servers and accessible only to the research team. Aggregated, anonymous results may be published in academic papers and shared with other researchers; individual responses will never be reported in a way that could identify you.

Anonymous data may be retained indefinitely for research replication and reuse.

For the submission of online survey responses, MIT reserves the right to withhold compensation and/or verify the identity of participants in the event it suspects multiple and/or fraudulent survey responses.

Voluntary Participation

Participation in this study is voluntary. You may decline to answer any or all questions, and you may withdraw from the study at any time without adverse consequences. Withdrawing will not affect your standing on Prolific.
